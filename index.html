Current thesis

The next frontier in government is not AI for its own sake. It is applied intelligence embedded into public systems: procurement, permitting, urban management, service delivery, land systems, census operations, health and disaster response, and the digital institutions that make states more capable.

What this site is for

A home for essays, books, and practical thinking on how cities and governments can become smarter, faster, and more equitable, without losing sight of political economy, implementation realities, or institutional design.

Focus Areas

Four themes that run through the work

AI for public service delivery

Using AI to improve operational decision-making, frontline delivery, early warning systems, and citizen-facing services.

Digital public infrastructure

Building the rails for identity, payments, data-sharing, consent, and government platforms that scale across sectors.

Cities, land, and resilience

Linking urban growth, housing, infrastructure, and risk management to the long-run performance of cities and regions.

Rewiring the machinery of government so strategy, institutions, incentives, and technology reinforce one another.

About

A global perspective on cities, government, and public systems

Abhas Jha brings more than two decades of global experience at the intersection of cities, public policy, finance, land, infrastructure, and institutional reform, with work spanning more than 40 countries across Asia, Africa, Latin America, and the Caribbean.

His perspective is grounded not only in research and global advisory work, but in hands-on experience with policymaking and implementation at the city, provincial, and national levels, helping governments translate ambition into workable systems, programs, and results.

His current writing focuses on how AI, digital public infrastructure, and stronger state capability can help governments deliver better public services, especially in rapidly urbanizing and institutionally complex environments.

Core lenses

  • Digital public infrastructure and governance
  • Urbanization, land, and housing
  • Resilience and disaster risk management
